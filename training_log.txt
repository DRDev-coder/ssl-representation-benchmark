  Batch size    : 128

  Probe epochs  : 100 per config (frozen encoder + linear classifier)
  Optimizer     : Adam (lr=3e-4)
  Configs tested: full, no_color_jitter, no_blur, no_grayscale, no_crop, crop_flip_only
  GPU           : NVIDIA GeForce RTX 5060 Laptop GPU (8.0 GB)

Loss at epoch 100 per config:
  full            : 4.2336
  no_color_jitter : 4.0882  (lower loss -- fewer augmentations = easier pretext task)
  no_blur         : 4.1725
  no_grayscale    : 4.0322
  no_crop         : 3.5954  (much lower -- no random crop = trivial positives)
  crop_flip_only  : 3.8299

Probe Accuracy Results:
  Config           | Accuracy | Delta vs full
  -----------------|----------|--------------
  no_blur          |   69.02% |    +0.90%
  full             |   68.12% |   baseline
  no_color_jitter  |   60.37% |    -7.75%
  no_grayscale     |   54.68% |   -13.44%
  crop_flip_only   |   46.61% |   -21.51%
  no_crop          |   38.69% |   -29.43%

Ranking (most critical augmentation):
  1. Random Resized Crop  -29.43%  << most important by far
  2. Grayscale            -13.44%
  3. Color Jitter          -7.75%
  4. Gaussian Blur         +0.90%  (removing blur HELPS on 32x32!)

Key Findings:
  1. Random crop is by far the most critical augmentation on CIFAR-10 (-29.4 pp).
     Without crop, two views are nearly identical (just H-flip), making the
     pretext task trivially easy and preventing diverse representation learning.

  2. Grayscale is second most important (-13.4 pp). Removing it allows
     the encoder to rely on colour shortcuts to match positive pairs, reducing
     the quality of spatial/texture features.

  3. Color jitter ranks third (-7.8 pp). Important, but not dominant on 32x32.

  4. Gaussian blur HURTS on CIFAR-10 (+0.9 pp better without it). At 32x32
     pixels, blurring destroys the limited spatial detail available. This
     contrasts with the SimCLR ImageNet paper (224x224) where blur helps.

Hypothesis H4 outcome:
  REFUTED. H4 predicted color jitter would be most critical (per Chen et al.
  2020 on ImageNet). On CIFAR-10 (32x32), random crop dominates. The
  resolution difference explains the divergence: at 32x32, crops create
  harder positive pairs (less pixel overlap), making crop-invariance the
  dominant learning objective.

Outputs saved:
  results/augmentation_ablation_results.json
  results/augmentation_ablation.png

  BYOL is 10.68 pp below SimCLR — well outside the threshold.
  SimCLR (67.56%) ≈ MoCo (65.47%) are close (2.09 pp ✓), but BYOL
  significantly underperforms, making H5 overall REFUTED.

Why BYOL underperformed at small batch:
  1. Batch size sensitivity: BYOL's bootstrap signal relies on a stable
     target network. At batch=128 (vs paper's 4096), the online network
     sees too few updates between target refreshes, weakening signal quality.
  2. Fixed EMA momentum: Original BYOL cosine-schedules momentum from
     0.996→1.0. Our fixed 0.996 means the target never fully stabilises
     in later epochs, allowing slight representation drift.
  3. No LARS optimiser: Original BYOL uses LARS with LR=0.2. Adam with
     LR=3e-4 may yield a suboptimal projection/predictor balance.
  4. Contrastive methods (SimCLR, MoCo) are inherently more robust at
     small batch because explicit negatives anchor the representation space
     regardless of batch size — BYOL has no such anchor.
